How much does $10,000 more down payment save?

Comparing 5% down ($15,000) to 10% down ($30,000) on this home: the extra $15,000 down lowers the monthly payment by about $153 — a combination of a smaller loan, a slightly better rate, and lower PMI. Whether that trade is worth it depends on what else that $15,000 could do for you (an emergency fund, other debt, moving costs).

Frequently asked questions

What income would I need for a $300,000 house?

Using a common guideline of housing payment at roughly 28% of gross monthly income, the 10%-down scenario above (~$2,260/mo) points to about $8,070/month, or roughly $96,800/year — though this varies by lender and your other debt obligations.

Does a 15-year loan change these numbers a lot?

Yes, significantly — a 15-year term raises the monthly principal and interest substantially in exchange for a much lower total interest cost. See our 15-year vs. 30-year comparison for the real math.
